# Hosting a Weaving Group

A guide for weavers — people hosting and guiding a group. For context on what
weaving groups are and who they're for, see the [README](./README.md).

## Forming Groups

There are a few ways for form groups - broadly you can pre-assign before the
gathering, or dynamically assign on the first day. We always organise weavers
(group hosts) before the event so there is some supported structure for people
to arrive into.

The Dynamics Assignment process we've run previously is to give participants
the following simple rules, and to let the physics of those rules transform the
group into a fluid which solves for "good shaped groups"

Rules
- Groups form around a Weaver
- Repel from people you already know (to the extent of your comfort)
- Ideally: 1 old hand, max 7 people

In 2026 we're going to seed a couple of dedicated groups:
- Portugeuse Langauge speaking
- Parents with your kids
- Gender Diverse

## Running a session

A weaving group can flow simply through four phases. Below are two recipes:
one for the first time you meet, and one for the days after.

### Recipe: First meeting

*Use this the first time the group gathers.*

1. **Context** — the weaver introduces themself and briefly explains what
   weaving groups are (e.g. this crew will meet daily, it's a chance to check
   in, answer questions, share a little).
2. **Introductions** — sit in a circle. The weaver starts a "round" (going
   first, then passing to their left), where each person answers 3 questions:
    1. what's your name?
    2. where did you travel here from?
    3. what's something you're passionate about / love to do?
3. **Free-form** 
    - any general questions about camp?
        - _(the weaver doesn't need to know everything — rather, help route the question)_ 
    - consider another question (see "Prompt round" questions below)
        - it could be as simple as _"How's camp going so far?"_
    - ask _"Anything else?"_
4. **Close**
    - remind people of the time + place tomorrow
    - clearly mark that the gathering is done (people are welcome to hang or head off)


### Recipe: Each day

*Use this for the meetings after the first.*

1. **Check-in** — sit in a circle. Do a round where each person speaks briefly
   to: "how are you feeling / how's your day going?" This helps people arrive
   in the group — settling, focusing, connecting — and gives everyone a chance
   to be heard. It can surface problems that need support too (address briefly,
   or loop back later).
2. **Prompt round** — do another round, inviting people to respond to a prompt
   for ~2 mins while others listen. Allow brief, curious questions but gently
   keep it moving. Example prompts:
    - what is one thing you loved from today?
    - what's the story of how you arrived here?
    - what's your relationship to the decentralized web?
    - what's a community that helped raise you?
    - where do you feel your attention is focused lately?
    - what questions are you reflecting on at the moment?
    - what is something unique to you that you bring to DWeb Camp?
    - what's something that people wouldn't know about you by looking at you?
    - what's something you ate last week that you really enjoyed?
    - how do you like to spend your energy?
    - what are you currently trying to unlearn?
    - what's a memory, image, smell, food or noise from your childhood that
      reminds you of home / safety?
    - what were you surprised by today / this camp?
    - what's a hill you'd die on?
3. **Free-form** — choose what best serves
    - can ask if any needs/ questions have surfaced
    - could pose another question, or to open up a thread that emerged during rounds,
      or just move to open conversation.
4. **Close**
    - remind people of the time + place tomorrow
    - clearly mark that the gathering is done.


### Weaver styles

Every weaver runs things a little differently — go with what feels right for
you and your group.

- **Pick a path** (Mix): once the structured part is done, name how much time is
  left and let the group choose how to spend it, e.g. 
    - (a) open conversation about something someone raised,
    - (b) pair walks — pair up, go for a 15 min walk and just talk, then head off to dinner, or 
    - (c) share what you're looking forward to tomorrow.
- **A clear close** matters — otherwise the energy tapers off, people drift,
  and it gets a little weird :P People can of course keep talking afterwards.
- **Cards** — a deck like "We're Not Really Strangers" can work well: each person
  picks a question, or uses one already on the table.

## FAQ - Weavers

1. **I don't know everything, what if someone asks a question I don't know how
to answer?**
    - Totally normal. You can say you don't know and but that you can help find the
      right person to ask
    - You might also ask the group - there's a decent chance there's another
      participant who knows.
2. **How do we manage conflict?**
    - This process is designed to be low-conflict, but if something does come up,
    it is not your responsibility to resolve it
    - If anything feels unsafe, you are welcome to close the Weaving group for
    the day,:
        - e.g. _"Given where we're at, I'm going to gently move us to close for
        today. People are welcome to stay and hang out, or to depart as suits
        them."_
        - Please bring bring anyone who feels at-risk for support from the Head
        Weaver or the team hosting the event
    - If it's just low level tension, you can remind everyone that people have
    travelled here from all over, so difference is expected. invite everyone to
    slow down, take space as needed, and if anyone needs support to reach out
    to the organisers.
3. **Where do we meet?**
    - Up to your group
    - Highly recommend you meet the same place each day, and that where you end up sitting as a group is not to far from there so anyone running late isn't completely lost
4. **How do I handle someone talking too much?!
    - This can happen because of cultural + personal differences in communication, and is totally normal
    - **During rounds**, if you get a lot of **replies**, or lengthy comments which are taking up too much space
        - _"For this round, I'd like us all focus on listening. These are some
        great reflections, but lets get around everyone and if we have time at
        the end we can revisit these"_"
        - Note that inviting people to do a "round" (where we each get a turn)
        is a structure designed to help spread out speaking.
    - **During free-form** conversations
        - _"I'd like to make sure we get a chance to hear everyone - does
        anyone we haven't heard from yet have something to add?"_
        - _"Lots of enthusisasm here! AND I notice it's mainly 2 people talking at
        the moment. I'd like to suggest a rule where people wait for two others to speak
        before speaking again"_
        - _"Today we're going to try a 'talking stick' - if you want to talk,
        you have to be holding this stick :D."_
